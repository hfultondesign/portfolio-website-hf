about me

my experience

My design career began over ten years ago with graphic design. After earning a Bachelor of Design from the Ontario College of Art & Design University, I worked closely with a wide array of clients (clothing designers, musicians, art galleries to name a few). I worked on a variety of projects with a focus on brand assets and promotional materials.

My graphic design work is what introduced me to the non- profit arts sector, where I spent eight years practicing design as my trade while also taking on other roles. I was the Operations Co-ordinator of an artist-run art gallery, dividing my time between designing materials for exhibitions and ensuring the day-to-day at the gallery ran smoothly. In addition to the administrative duties, I used my DIY skills to hang artwork and build gallery furniture (I even built a bar!).

my values

be curious

I excel as an auto-didact. I learned the foundational fundamentals of graphic design at university, but every other skill I can offer is self-taught. I am naturally very self-motivated and am always eager to pick up and hone new skills.

stay creative

In addition to my design work, I maintain an active art practice. I'm a photo-based printmaker specializing in historical photographic processes. Engaging with my art practice not only keeps me sane, but keeps my creativity sharp and readily available to take advantage of in my design work.

adapt, adapt, adapt

Being resourceful with restrictive budgets and timelines, dealing with unforeseeable challenges, and working through barriers are all skills I've developed through great experience working as a graphic designer and working within the nonprofit sector. I'm able to approach projects with a plan in mind that is built to be able to pivot as needed.
