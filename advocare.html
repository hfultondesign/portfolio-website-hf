Advocare

Overview

Advocare is a mobile app designed for users who experience chronic health problems. Navigating the health care system and tracking illness progression can be exhausting, but is often necessary for users with chronic health conditions. For many who are already debilitated by their health problems, this can take a huge toll on their already limited energy; physically, mentally and emotionally.

Role

I designed Advocare, taking the role of Product Designer/UX Designer. This project was created for Career Foundry's UX Immersion course.

Defining the Problem

Problem Statement

Users with Chronic Health Conditions need a way to better advocate for themselves when accessing health care because their ability to communicate with their care providers is compromised by:

  1. the energy needed to manage their current symptoms and treatment, and;
  2. systemic barriers within the healthcare system

Proposed Solution

Visit Tracker

A tool for users to log upcoming and past visits with doctors, specialists, therapists, and other health care providers. Users can also store location and contact information for their providers, making it all accessible in one place.

Visit Prep

The ability to create a Visit Plan, where users can prepare for doctor appointments by preparing a list of questions and discussion points in advance. Users can set goals for the visit to ensure they make the most of their time with their provider.

Visit Summary

A tool for users to record how their visit went. Users can confirm which of their visit goals were met, and record important notes from what was discussed. They can also use this tool to keep track of treatment changes made.

User Research

User Survey

111 users responded to a survey hosted on Google Forms. Participants were sourced from various online forums for people with chronic health issues.

Self Advocacy

  • Systemic barriers and challenges navigating health care systems are widely reported
  • People living with chronic health issues are susceptible to being misunderstood and have their serious concerns dismissed

Common consequences of systemic health care barriers:

  • delayed diagnosis & treatment
  • significant and unnecessary medical debt
  • stress and trauma from repeated medical gaslighting
  • preventable illness progression

User Needs

Users were asked: Which of these tasks are relevant or necessary to managing your health condition?

Results indicate the majority of users must interact regularly with health care providers (doctors, specialists, therapists, imaging technicians, etc)

User Satisfaction of Competitor Products

Users were asked:How helpful is/are the app(s) you've used in managing your chronic health condition?

  • 50% rated the helpfulness of these apps at 3/5 or below
  • Only 13% of users rated the apps at 5/5
  • Results show a need and opportunity for improvement of user experience

    AHRQ QuestionBuilder

    A simple and stripped down product - only two very basic features. Users can organize care team visits and prepare a list of questions to address. Developed in collaboration with healthcare professionals.

    FlareDown

    Aligns themselves as a medical app, and use a comprehensive symptom tracker feature as a marketing focal point. Since its’ inception in 2014 and has had a lot of time to fine tune their design. FlareDown was developed using crowd-sourced funding.

    Design Iterations

    Treatments

    Originally, I had designed a treatment tracking feature. After streamlining the design, and focusing on fewer features, I still wanted users to be able to access information about their treatments.

    Dashboard

    Early iterations included pages called Feedboards, which served as a Dashboard for a specific health condition the user is tracking. Users would have one per condition they track.   As I streamlined my design, I realized this created a navigation experience that was convoluted. As the design progressed I switched to a single dedicated Dashboard that would include information relevant to all conditions the user is tracking.

    Care Visits

    Early iterations had one section of the app called Care. This section featured three sub sections: Care Visits, Care Team, and Care Clinics. I later re-organized my information architecture in order to streamline the UX. All information pertaining to Care Clinics is found on Care Provider profile pages. The Care Team subsection is now a child of the more generalized Visits parent section.   Visits that occurred in the past are now viewed by tapping the Visit History link, which helped trim down the excess of information on this page.
